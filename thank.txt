3. TIMELINE PAGE
Banner:
* Title: Timeline of the Nigerian Armed Forces
* Blurb: From colonial beginnings to modern operations, a story of courage, unity, and service - remembering past sacrifices and ongoing commitment.
* Tagline: A journey through history, written in courage.
Intro for Timeline Display:
Explore the defining moments of the Nigerian Armed Forces - a story of heroism, service, and dedication that continues to shape Nigeria's destiny.
Chronological Timeline:
1900-1945: Early Formations & Global War Service
* 1900: Establishment of the West African Frontier Force (WAFF).
* 1914-1918: Nigerian soldiers participate in WWI campaigns.
* 1939-1945: Nigerian troops serve in WWII in North Africa, Burma, and Europe.
1956-1960: Transition to Independence
* Mid-1950s: Expansion of the Nigerian Regiment; local command increases.
* 1960: Nigeria gains independence; Armed Forces symbolize national unity.
1967-1970: Nigerian Civil War (Biafra Conflict)
* July 1967: Start of hostilities; Operation UNICORD launched.
* Armed Forces play a critical role in preserving national unity.
1970-1979: Post-War Reconstruction & Peacekeeping Beginnings
* Rebuilding and professionalisation of Army, Navy, and Air Force.
* Nigeria contributes to UN Operation in the Congo (ONUC).
* New units and command structures established for international deployments.
1980-1989: Expansion and Regional Peacekeeping
* Development of specialised peacekeeping units and training centres.
1990-1999: Professionalisation and African Leadership
* Leading role in African peacekeeping; deployments to Liberia and Sierra Leone.
* Formation of specialised contingents for multinational operations.
2000-2010: Modern Operations and Regional Security
* Internal security challenges: insurgency, maritime security, and humanitarian operations.
* 2003-2018: Contributions to UNMIL (Liberia) peacekeeping.
* 2004: Deployment of the first Formed Police Unit (FPU) to Liberia.
2011-Present: Modernisation and Multinational Operations
* Counter-insurgency operations and regional coalition efforts strengthened.
* Formation and deployment of Multinational Joint Task Force (MNJTF) against Boko Haram.
* 2013: Deployment to AFISMA (Mali) regional security mission.
* Nigeria continues contributions to over 40 UN/AU/ECOWAS missions globally.
